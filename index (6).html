About

I am a research assistant in the Department of Electronics Engineering at Pusan National University (B.S. '26), advised by Prof. Sangkil Kim.

Outside the lab, you'll find me swimming, lifting, making coffee, or exploring electronics just for the fun of understanding how they work. I'm just as curious about what makes a good life as I am about what the universe is really up to.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of wireless communications, sensing, and artificial intelligence — with the goal of building next-generation wireless systems that can simultaneously communicate, sense, and learn from their environment.

Projects

Neural Network-Based Channel Estimation

Exploring deep learning approaches for channel estimation and signal recovery in backscatter and low-SNR wireless systems, where model-based methods face fundamental performance floors.

PyTorchPythonMATLAB

Phase-Aware Channel Estimation for Backscatter Links

Proposed lifted LS/LMMSE estimators that jointly resolve composite channel gain and residual phase drift, achieving up to 27.8 dB NMSE suppression with O(N) complexity.

MATLABPythonSignal Processing

Ultra-Low-Power Monostatic Backscatter Platform

Built an end-to-end monostatic backscatter link with semi-passive tag (10 μW), SDR reader, and full signal processing chain. Achieved zero-error image transmission at 500 kbps.

GNU RadioUSRP N200PCB DesignFPGA

Backscatter Range Extension via Reflection Amplifiers

Designed an active tag with reflection amplifier and 90° hybrid coupler, achieving |Γ| > 1 and extending reliable OOK detection to 2.5 m.

ADSAnsys HFSSVNASDR
