About

I am a research assistant in the Department of Electronics Engineering at Pusan National University (B.S. '26), advised by Prof. Sangkil Kim.

Outside the lab, you'll find me swimming, lifting, making coffee, or exploring electronics just for the fun of understanding how they work. I'm just as curious about what makes a good life as I am about what the universe is really up to.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of wireless communications, sensing, and artificial intelligence — with the goal of building next-generation wireless systems that can simultaneously communicate, sense, and learn from their environment.

I am broadly interested in the following areas:

Backscatter CommunicationsChannel Modeling & EstimationRF/Antenna DesignSoftware-Defined RadioAI/DL for Wireless CommunicationsWireless SensingIoT Systems

1. Ultra-Low-Power Backscatter Communication Systems

I design and build monostatic backscatter platforms through hardware–software co-design. My recent work integrates a semi-passive tag, an SDR-based reader, and a high-isolation planar antenna to deliver reliable OOK/BPSK multimedia transmission at 500 kbps with 20 pJ/bit tag-side energy efficiency.

2. AI-Driven Wireless Signal Processing

I am exploring deep learning-based approaches for channel estimation and signal recovery under severe hardware constraints, where conventional model-based methods encounter fundamental performance floors.

Projects

Monostatic Backscatter Multimedia Platform

End-to-end monostatic system with OOK/BPSK modulation, DC offset removal, CFO compensation, and ZF equalization. Achieved BER < 10⁻⁷ and zero-error full-color image reconstruction over the air.

MATLABGNU RadioUSRP N200PCB

Semi-Passive Tag & High-Isolation Antenna

Designed a backscatter tag with ATmega328P MCU and HMC221BE SPDT switch (10 μW RF-side), paired with a 2×1 planar Yagi-Uda array. SRRs suppress Tx–Rx coupling to < −30 dB at 2.45 GHz.

HFSSADSOrCADVNA

Phase-Aware Channel Estimation

Proposed lifted LS/LMMSE estimators that jointly resolve composite channel gain and residual phase drift. Achieves up to 27.8 dB NMSE suppression over conventional methods while preserving O(N) complexity.

MATLABPythonSignal Processing

Backscatter Range Extension via Reflection Amplifiers

Designed an active tag using a reflection amplifier with 90° hybrid coupler, achieving |Γ| > 1 and 1.5× signal amplitude gain. Extended reliable detection range to 2.5 m with OOK modulation.
