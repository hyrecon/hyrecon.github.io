Outside the lab, you'll find me swimming, lifting, making coffee, or exploring electronics just for the fun of understanding how they work. I'm just as curious about what makes a good life as I am about what the universe is really up to.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of wireless communications, sensing, and artificial intelligence — with the goal of building next-generation wireless systems that can simultaneously communicate, sense, and learn from their environment.

I am broadly interested in the following areas:

Backscatter CommunicationsChannel Modeling & EstimationRF/Antenna DesignSoftware-Defined RadioAI/DL for Wireless CommunicationsWireless SensingIoT Systems

1. Backscatter Communication System Design

I develop end-to-end monostatic backscatter platforms through hardware–software co-design, integrating custom tag circuitry, high-isolation antenna systems, and SDR-based signal processing pipelines to enable scalable, energy-efficient IoT connectivity.

2. Channel Estimation under Hardware Impairments

I design estimation frameworks that explicitly account for residual phase drifts and cascaded fading in backscatter links, achieving robust channel recovery where conventional estimators exhibit irreducible error floors.

3. AI-Driven Wireless Signal Processing

I am exploring deep learning-based approaches for channel estimation and signal recovery in hardware-constrained wireless systems, targeting scenarios where model-based methods face fundamental limitations.

Projects

Ultra-Low-Power Monostatic Backscatter Multimedia Communication

Built a complete monostatic backscatter link — from a semi-passive tag and SDR reader to a full signal processing chain (DC removal, CFO compensation, phase-aware CE, ZF equalization). Demonstrated zero-error full-color image reconstruction over the air at BER < 10⁻⁷.

Phase-Aware Channel Estimation for Backscatter Links

Proposed lifted two-parameter LS/LMMSE estimators that jointly resolve the composite channel gain and residual phase slope via first-order linearization, suppressing NMSE by up to 27.8 dB with closed-form solutions.

MATLABPythonStatistical Signal Processing

Backscatter Range Extension via Reflection Amplifiers

Designed an active backscattering tag using a reflection amplifier with 90° hybrid coupler, achieving |Γ| > 1 and 1.5× signal amplitude gain. Extended reliable OOK detection range to 2.5 m.
